## Team

A three-person team out of Manchester and Southampton. Two cooks in the kitchen, one running the front of house.

* **Andrew Robertson** (Manchester CS, final year). The backend, the AI orchestration, the deploy, and the ad-tech voice in the pitch. FastAPI + in-memory datastore. The 8-rule slice advisor that produces the daily action queue. Chat orchestrator with typed tool calls, so every number the assistant cites comes from a real Python function instead of a hallucination. Gemma agents for variant briefs and vision insights. The rotating API-key pool with exponential-backoff retry that keeps the demo alive on free-tier Gemini. Vercel and Render deploys, with smadex.cooking pointed through Porkbun. Four months at The Trade Desk gave him the creative-metrics and DSP-integration intuition that lets the pitch land as a Smadex internal feature, not a hackathon toy. Co-founder of Basanite.

* **Krish Mathur** (Southampton CS, first year). The math and the model. Fatigue classifier from changepoint feature engineering through to a held-out, campaign-grouped evaluation: the result beats the dataset's own `ctr_decay_pct` baseline by 6 ROC-AUC points (0.93 vs 0.87). Tiered fatigue verdicts that surface in the cockpit with confidence and a predicted fatigue day. Composite Health KPI scoring across six dimensions. The acronym-tooltip layer that makes CTR, CVR, ROAS, IPM, CPI, CPM, KPI, ATT, DSP, MMP, MMM, and LTV legible at first glance for any non-marketer reading the cockpit. ICHack26 winner pace applied to a 36-hour build.

* **Aditya Shah** (Manchester CS, final year). The pitch, the demo, and the verification net. Walks judges through the 3-minute story twice in one weekend, once at A3 for HackUPC general judging and once at A4 for Smadex sponsor judging, both without slides because the brief doesn't allow them. Owns the checklist that catches every "actually that's broken in production" before a judge does. Prepares GIF fallbacks for every demo step in case the venue Wi-Fi or Render free tier blink at the wrong moment. CEO of Basanite, 500+ hours of STEM tutoring, 13-month BigQuery placement at Virgin Media O2. He has seen marketers actually use tools like this one, and the demo is tuned accordingly.

Aditya doesn't write the code; he writes the room. That is its own discipline, and it is why the same project gets two clean demos in two different judging rooms instead of one rushed one.
